    Introduction

    Pineapple (Ananas comosus) is one of Goa's commercial fruit crops. It originated in Brazil and later spread to other tropical parts of the world. Pineapple cultivation is limited to heavy rainfall and humid coastal regions on the Indian mainland and in mountainous areas. In Goa it is grown on an area of 336 hectares with an annual production of 5,040 tons. Pineapples grow well in humid tropical conditions, both in the coastal belts and inland. The optimal temperature is 22 to 32 ° C. Can be grown up to 1100m above sea level when there is no frost. Optimal rainfall of 100-150cm is required, but nevertheless pineapples thrive in areas with high rainfall and also in dry areas with additional protective irrigation during the dry season. It can grow well in any type of soil except heavy loam. Sandy loam is ideal for pineapples.A soil 4560 cm deep with no stale bread or stones and a pH of 5.06.0 is preferred.

    Propagation Method for Pineapple Planting:

    Pineapple is very easy to propagate vegetatively and through suckers that form in the axils of the leaves. Slips, which are suckers that emerge immediately beneath the fruit, are another method of propagation. Suckers and slips, on the other hand, must be preferred for planting because they bear earlier than the crown and produce larger fruits. After 3 to 20 years, pineapple plants from the crown bear flowers.

    The plant's performance is determined by its vigour, growth rate, time required to bear fruit, and the quality of the planting material. Aside from the type and size of plant material, there is also variation in the performance of Pineapple plants. When different types and sizes of planting material are used, it results in a low rate of plant establishment, uneven growth of the Pineapple plants, and uneven flotation.

    Ultimately, plant-wise operations will be followed, resulting in increased production costs in Pineapple. In a mixed planting, a few Pineapple plants flower while others mature, posing challenges to getting good uniform ratoon crops. As a result, it is always best to use uniform-sized monotype material. As a result, selecting the appropriate type and size of planting mater is critical.

    Larger planting material in suckers and slips results in more vigorous Pineapple plants. Slips and suckers weighing about 350 and 450 grammes, respectively, are ideal for higher crop yield with better productivity among the various types and sizes of propagules tested. In the absence of slips, suckers weighing around 500 grammes are ideal for planting. In the absence of suckers, slips are used..

    Irrigation

    Pineapples are grown along coastlines and in areas with high rainfall. As a result, irrigation is unnecessary. However, when grown on a commercial scale, supplemental irrigation aids in the production of large fruits. Irrigation also aids in off-season planting. It also ensures Pineapple production all year. This method is used to produce high-quality pineapple fruits for export. Although the pineapple crop is primarily rain-fed, it is heavily irrigated. Drip irrigation is used successfully in areas where water supply is limited, labour costs are high, and cultivation methods are advanced.
